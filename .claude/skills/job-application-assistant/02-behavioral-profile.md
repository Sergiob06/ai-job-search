## Overview
<!-- Self-assessment gathered via /setup questions, not a formal instrument (PI/DISC/MBTI). Treat as directional, not clinical. -->
Sergio's self-described working style is best summarized as **adaptable and risk-calibrated**: he doesn't have one fixed mode of working, but reads the situation - structured when a task calls for it, autonomous when it doesn't - and matches his decision speed to the stakes involved.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Structure vs. Autonomy | Balanced | No fixed preference - adapts to what the task requires rather than needing one mode consistently |
| Decision Speed | Risk-calibrated | Moves fast on low-stakes/reversible decisions, slows down and researches for high-stakes ones |
| Communication | Direct + documented | Clear and to the point verbally, but also values written documentation of decisions and requirements |
| Growth Orientation | High | Motivated by learning new frameworks, mentorship, and regular feedback rather than routine alone |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **Context-sensitive adaptability:** Shifts naturally between structured guidance and self-directed work depending on what the task calls for.
- **Calibrated risk-taking:** Ships quickly on reversible, low-stakes work; slows down to research and plan when the stakes are higher.
- **Clear, dual-mode communicator:** Comfortable discussing ideas live in meetings, and equally comfortable documenting decisions and technical details in writing.

## How You Work Best
- Priorities that are reasonably clear at the outset, even if they evolve over time - not rigid, but not directionless either.
- Regular feedback loops, especially valuable at this early career stage.
- A mix of new feature/product work and enough maintenance to stay grounded in how the product actually works - not maintenance as the entire job.

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Independent judgment on ambiguous, high-stakes calls:** Still early career - actively seeks mentorship to build calibrated technical judgment faster, rather than avoiding ambiguity.
- **Breadth of production experience:** One 6-month internship plus a personal project so far - frame as fast-ramping (delivered across two parallel technical tracks during the internship) rather than as a limitation.

## Mapping to Job Posting Language

When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "Mentorship", "growth opportunities", "learning and development"
- "Collaborative team", "cross-functional collaboration"
- "Feedback culture", "regular check-ins", "code reviews"
- "Clear requirements" or "evolving roadmap" (both are fine - not rigid on this)

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not deal-breaker):
- "Fully autonomous, minimal oversight" for a junior-level role (mismatch with current need for mentorship, not a hard block)
- "Maintenance of legacy systems" as the sole listed responsibility, with no new development mentioned
- "Fast-paced, constantly shifting priorities" with no mention of structure or planning

## Management Style Preferences
- Works well with managers who give clear initial direction, then extend ownership as trust builds, paired with regular feedback.
- Does not work well with purely command-and-control management offering no room to learn, or fully hands-off management with zero structure or feedback for a junior developer.

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Weave in the two-track internship (parallel Angular/Spring Boot development and WordPress SEO/WPO optimisation) as evidence of adaptability across different types of technical work.
- **CV:** Emphasize both the internship (structured, team-based) and the Sports Management Laravel project (self-directed, full ownership) to show range.
- **Interviews:** Use the Laravel project STAR stub for autonomy/ownership questions; use the internship STAR stubs for collaboration and versatility questions.
